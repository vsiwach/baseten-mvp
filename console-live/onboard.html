Hardware ladder (8B-class; re-rank live for other sizes)

      
From skills/baseten-onboard/references/decision-table.md — proven
         SKUs over theoretically-optimal ones.

      
    
        
  • 1 · T4x8x32 — proven end-to-end in this repo:
          schedulable, 16 GiB VRAM fits 8B-AWQ + 4k KV, warm TTFT 300–333 ms,
          activation→ready 114 s warm-cache, cold model.load()
          148 s with the BDN weights block (evidence: evals/mcp-deploy/PHASE1_EVIDENCE.md
          + docs/FRICTION_LOG.md #17; price: fetch live above).
    • 
        
  • 2 · L4-class — more VRAM / newer; verify
          schedulability in your org first.
    • 
        
  • 3 · A10G — known
          org-gating risk: rejected at truss push,
          not at config validation (friction #13). Only take with a fallback ranked.
    • 
        
  • Avoid single-T4 SKUs with ≤16 GiB system RAM
          for 8B — host-OOM crash-loop observed (friction #7).
    • 
      

      
Config non-negotiables

      
    
        
  • weights: BDN block — measured 360 s → 148 s
          cold start, 2.4× (friction #17).
    • 
        
  • Fully pinned requirements — unpinned transformers caused an import-time
          crash-loop with green builds (friction #14:
          vllm==0.9.1 needs
          transformers==4.53.2).
    • 
        
  • Org-gating means: rank a fallback, never promise a SKU before push
          succeeds (friction #13).

When the agent finishes

      
The skill's last step hands you a deep link:
        https://baseten-reliability-console.vercel.app/?model=<model_id> —
        it opens the fleet console focused on your new deployment's card (outlined
        for a few seconds), with live SLO posture, traffic, and gated manage
        actions. One workload's metrics live on Baseten's own dashboard
        (app.baseten.co/models/<model_id>/overview);
        the console earns its keep when you run more than one, or hold an SLO.
